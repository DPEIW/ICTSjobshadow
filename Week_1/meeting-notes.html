Meeting Notes - 8th May 2024

Topics Discussed:

  • Gravitational-Wave Selection Effects: Analysis of how selection bias impacts the observation of gravitational waves.
  • Lensing: Discussion on gravitational lensing and its role in detecting and analyzing distant astrophysical phenomena.
  • Black Hole Mergers: Updates on recent findings regarding the dynamics and properties of merging black holes.

Key Points:

- Importance of addressing selection effects to ensure accuracy in gravitational-wave data interpretation.

- Challenges in observing lensed gravitational waves and distinguishing them from direct signals.

- Computational techniques for simulating black hole mergers and interpreting their gravitational wave signatures.

Additional Notes:

- Discussed current research gaps and potential future studies in gravitational wave analysis.

- Shared insights on using supercomputing resources to simulate astrophysical events more efficiently.
